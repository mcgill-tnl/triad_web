Our research occurs at three different sites.

McGill Centre for Studies in Aging (MCSA) is a victorian house situated at the grounds of the Douglas Hospital (Verdun). When entering the Douglas grounds directly off of Lasalle Boulevard it is found at the second driveway on the right.

Address: 6825 Boulevard LaSalle, Verdun

What is done here:

  • Clinical visits
  • Data analysis

Crossroads is an annex house of the MCSA also located at the grounds of the Douglas Hospital (Verdun). When entering the Douglas grounds directly off of Lasalle boulevard it is found at the first driveway on the right.

Address: 6875 Boulevard LaSalle, Verdun

What is done here:

  • Clinical assessment
  • Physical and neurological examination
  • Cognitive and Neuropsychological exams
  • Collecting fluid samples (blood, urine or saliva)
  • Lumbar puncture (spinal tap) if you are having this done

Montreal Neurological Institute (also called the MNI or “The Neuro”)

Address: 3801 Rue Université, Montréal

What is done here:

  • All of the PET studies
  • MRI scan

To get the PET and MRI scans done you will arrive to the MNI and be met by a member of our research team who will guide you to the correct location and be with you through the process.
